## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of **Carbon Brainprint**, pledge to create a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful community. We commit to fostering an environment where everyone‚Äîregardless of background, identity, or experience‚Äîfeels safe and valued.

Additionally, as this project is dedicated to **sustainability** and reducing **carbon footprints**, we encourage contributors to adopt eco-conscious practices in their contributions and discussions.

## Our Standards

To ensure a positive experience for all participants, we expect contributors to:

-   **Be respectful** and considerate in all interactions.
-   **Encourage collaboration** and open discussions.
-   **Give constructive feedback** that is helpful and supportive.
-   **Respect differing viewpoints** and experiences.
-   **Use inclusive language** and avoid offensive remarks.
-   **Help maintain a harassment-free environment** for everyone.
-   **Consider environmental science when sharing resources, recommending tools, or discussing methodologies.**

Unacceptable behaviors include:

-   **Harassment**, discrimination, or offensive comments based on gender, sexual orientation, disability, race, religion, or identity.
-   **Personal attacks, trolling, or inflammatory remarks**.
-   **Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or unwanted attention**.
-   **Publishing private information** (e.g., emails, addresses) without consent.
-   **Disruptive behavior** that hinders constructive discussions.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the maintainers at **sustainability@mantu.com**. All complaints will be reviewed and handled fairly.

## Consequences for violations may include:

-   A formal warning.
-   Temporary or permanent ban from contributing.
-   Reporting the incident to relevant authorities (if required).

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all contributions and interactions within the **Carbon Brainprint** community, including GitHub repositories, issue discussions, pull requests, social media, and any community events.
